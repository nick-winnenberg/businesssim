Food Truck Rally Consent Form

Welcome!

We appreciate your intrest in the Food Truck Simulation. We are using a virtual consent form. Please read and complete the form below!


Title of Research: Business Simulations in Entrepreneurship Education
Researchers: Nick Winnenberg (nw454410@ohio.edu), Dr. Luke Pittaway (pittaway@ohio.edu)

IRB number: 23-X-94

Once you have read this form and your questions about the study are answered, you will be asked to indicate your consent by clicking “I consent” before participating in this study.

Purpose of Study

This study is being done because there is a great opportunity for experiential learning in entrepreneurship. While traditional business coursework is functional role-heavy (marketing, accounting, management), entrepreneurship has a wider breadth. The research aims to help identify the best format for business simulations from an entrepreneurship education perspective.

You are being asked to participate in this study because we are reaching out to a wide audience with different business education and professional experiences across multiple industries.

Study Procedures

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to create a fiction food truck by analyzing different market segments, name and craft your business identity, select a menu for your food truck, then run through 10 separate “rounds”. During each round you can make different strategic decisions, including hiring/firing employees, investing in marketing, increasing/decreasing prices, etc.

This simulation game typically takes between 20-40 minutes; if you do not have time for the simulation, please revisit another time. Before progressing with the survey, then ultimately the simulation, you’ll be asked to create an account to access the simulation, this include an email address and password.

The simulation flow is as follows:

  1. Survey Marketing Demographic Information on Fictional Food Truck Rally Food Truck Identity Creation (Name, Food Type, Etc.)
  2. Menu Ingredient Selection
  3. Food Truck Selection
  4. Player Dashboard
  5. Then after 10 rounds, you will be presented with a leaderboard looking at the high score of your food truck.

This leaderboard will not have any identifiable information other than the name of your food truck.

Risks and Discomforts You May Experience

Risks or discomforts that you might experience are data-loss or breach of confidentiality. The information we are gathering is an email, years of professional experience, years of business education experience, job title, industry, and gender. Everything else gathered is fictional. Please do not provide any additional information.

Possible Benefits

This study is important to science/society because it allows us to better craft entrepreneurship education programs. Likewise, it allows businesses to create better decisions and lays a foundation for how to simulate real-world markets.

Individually, you may benefit as this is a fun game to rank your business results against others!

Confidentiality and Records

The information below describes how the confidentiality of your research records will be protected in this study. All the information is stored in a protected database to which only the software administrators have access. There is a very small chance of any data breach occurring. If it does, no sensitive or private information is gathered.

Additionally, while every effort will be made to keep your study-related information confidential, there may be circumstances where this information must be shared with:

  • Federal agencies, for example the Office of Human Research Protections, whose responsibility is to protect human subjects in research.
  • Representatives of Ohio University (OU), including the Institutional Review Board, a committee that oversees the research at OU.

Cost to Participate in the Research

There is no cost to you for participating in this research.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation in this research is completely voluntary, and you may leave the study at any time. If you decide to stop participating in the study, there will be no penalty to you or your relationship with Ohio University, and you will not lose any benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.

Withdrawal from Study

The investigators conducting the study may need to remove you from the study for a number of possible reasons, such as failure to follow directions or to protect you from excessive risk. If you leave the study early, either by voluntarily withdrawing or because the investigators remove you, data you provided up to that point will remain in the study database unless you explicitly request to withdraw your identifiable data.
